Founded by the Romans as Londinium around AD 43, London is now the capital and largest city of England and the UK. London serves as a global hotspot and a top tourist destination. London is considered to be one of the world's most important global cities, filled to the brim with culture, history, and lots of good food!

The Tate Modern

Sunday to Thursday: 10.00-18.00 Bankside, London Free
Friday to Saturday: 10.00-22.00

The Tate Modern is a modern art gallery located in Bankside, London. Tate holds the national collection of British art from 1900 to the present, along with many pieces of international modern and contemporary art! Lots of very cool pieces to view and all in a very interesting building. There is also an excellent viewing area at the top, where you can see most of London!

Oxford Street

Oxford Street is a major road in the City of Westminster. It is considered to be the biggest shopping street area in Europe. There are approximately 300 shops with lots of things to see and do in the area!

Some of the shops on Oxford Street include, but are not limited to:

  • Adidas
  • Massimo Dutti
  • Mango
  • Samsung
  • Vodafone
  • Nike
  • and many, many more...

The British Museum

The British Museum, in the Bloomsbury area, is a human history, art, and culture museum. It has a permanent collection of about eight million works, regarded as the largest and most comprehensive in existence. Most items were able to be widely sourced during the era of the British Empire. It is, in my opinion, one of the coolest museums in the world. The most notable item on display here is the Rosetta Stone!

City of Westminster

The City of Westminster is now in Central London but was the former capital of the Kingdom of England. It is a government district and contains several of the city's major attractions:

  • The Palace of Westminster
  • Big Ben
  • Buckingham Palace
  • Westminster Abbey

The area contains much to see and do, I highly recommend just walking around and embracing an adventurous spirit!

Buckingham Palace

Buckingham Palace is the London residence and administrative headquarters of the monarch of the United Kingdom. It is within the City of Westminster. The gardens that are nearby are very nice to walk through and the surrounding area serves as a great place to stop for afternoon tea! Notable to see is the Changing of the Guard.
